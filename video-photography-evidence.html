Photography Basics

My theme with these photos was for them to give out a colourful modern city feeling which also allowed me to explore the beautiful city that Manchester has become. I took a trip to Media City on a grey but bright day so I could really capture all of the vibrant colours that the buildings have to offer. The futuristic all glass buildings really give off a futuristic.I adjusted the exposure and offset to ensure my images had a nice popping colour that wasn’t too bright on the eyes since some of the images were taken on a bright day. Then I adjusted the RGB levels on the picture so the colours are vibrant with a soothing darker tone. I also upped the vibrance and the saturation levels so the colours on the buildings really give your eyes a warm feeling. When taking photos it is also important to think about the angles and framing of the object you're taking photos of (Photography Composition, 2012) . I made sure that the media city buildings fit the whole frame so the focal point is the building itself, it really makes sure that the buildings are the main focus in the picture. All the shots are landscape to ensure that the whole structure fits the frame. Whilst in editing I lowered the brightness on most images and upped the contrast to make sure the structures really stand out from the background. I then proceeded to adjust the vibrance levels as well as the hue and saturation to make colours dominate the picture.

Video Production Basics

Our group video task was to make an entertaining piece of work that would fit the vibe of MOTDx which is a spin off of the original Match of The Day. It targets teenagers and young adults. The idea for the video was to visit parks and neighbourhoods in Manchester and get the public's opinion on the current state of football. The name for the show would be “Street Talk”. We wanted to show what people really think about football by approaching them with an open and comedic attitude whilst also giving them some camera time to do something funny like a hidden talent. We shot in numerous locations such as Alexandra Park and Moss Side. My colleague Jakob Cotton was manning the camera and we decided to set the aspect ratio for television, which is 16:9. We filmed the whole day using a JVC Ultra HD camera. In the professional world mistakes can happen and unfortunately the raw camera footage that was captured was completely overexposed. I had taken on the roll early on to edit the footage and I felt a strike of stress flow through my body as I watched the footage for the first time. I dragged the footage into davinci and fixed some of the issues using the curves feature. However the footage was permanently damaged and made bright colours very noisy, may be due to the ISO setting being too high.
